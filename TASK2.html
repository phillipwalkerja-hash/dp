ARTICLE ONE:

Gov’t Committed to Tackling HIV/AIDS-Related Stigma
https://jis.gov.jm/govt-committed-to-tackling-hiv-aids-related-stigma/

Summary

        The articles present both progress and ongoing challenges in addressing HIV/AIDS in Jamaica. Juliet Cuthbert-Flynn highlights government commitment to reducing stigma and improving access to healthcare, including support for global anti-discrimination initiatives. However, they also reveal rights violations, particularly discrimination in healthcare settings, which undermines the right to health, equality, and dignity. Social stigma discourages vulnerable groups from seeking testing and treatment, reflecting broader societal inequality. While policies align with international human rights frameworks, gaps in implementation show that systemic barriers and prejudice continue to limit equitable access to care.

ARTICLE TWO:

Scorn and prejudice People living with HIV still being denied jobs, housing in Jamaica, says JASL
https://www.jamaicaobserver.com/2024/12/08/scorn-and-prejudice/

Summary

        The article highlight persistent discrimination against people living with HIV in Jamaica despite progress in awareness and treatment. Reports from the Jamaica AIDS Support for Life reveal ongoing violations of fundamental human rights, including denial of housing, employment, education, and social inclusion. These actions breach principles of equality and dignity, and expose gaps in legal protection, as health status is not constitutionally safeguarded. Evidence from stigma reports and real cases underscores systemic prejudice. Social stigma, fear of disclosure, and weak legislation continue to limit justice, demonstrating the urgent need for stronger legal frameworks and sustained advocacy.
